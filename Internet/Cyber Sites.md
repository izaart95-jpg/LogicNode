# Cybersecurity & Threat Intelligence Platforms Overview

This document provides a structured overview of major cybersecurity news sites, research groups, intelligence firms, underground forums, and communities.

---

# 1. Social & Community Platforms

## Discord
- Real-time chat platform
- Used by cybersecurity communities, bug bounty groups, OSINT teams
- Also abused by threat actors for C2 and malware distribution

## Reddit
- Subreddits like:
  - r/netsec
  - r/cybersecurity
  - r/ReverseEngineering
- Used for knowledge sharing and research discussions

## Telegram
- Encrypted messaging platform
- Widely used by:
  - Threat actors
  - Leak groups
  - Ransomware affiliates
  - OSINT researchers

## Dread
- Dark web Reddit-style forum
- Accessible via Tor
- Used for underground discussions and illicit trade

---

# 2. Security Conferences

## DEF CON
- One of the world's largest hacker conferences
- Focus on offensive security, hardware hacking, research
- Held annually in Las Vegas

## Infocon
- Refers to DEF CON’s threat level indicator
- Also used informally for cybersecurity awareness alerts

---

# 3. Cybersecurity News Websites

## Bleeping Computer
- Malware news
- Ransomware coverage
- Windows security
- Incident reporting

## The Hacker News
- Daily cybersecurity news
- Vulnerability disclosures
- Security tool updates

## Krebs on Security
- Investigative cybersecurity journalism
- Focus on cybercrime and data breaches
- Founded by Brian Krebs

## Cybersecurity News
- Aggregated security incident coverage
- Enterprise-focused

## SecurityWeek
- Enterprise security news
- Vulnerabilities
- Industry updates

## BBC (Technology Section)
- Covers major cyber incidents
- Consumer-facing security coverage

## Dark Reading
- Enterprise security analysis
- Threat intelligence
- Security strategy

## ZDNet (Security Section)
- Business technology news
- Cybersecurity product coverage

## TechCrunch (Security Section)
- Startup security news
- Data breach investigations
- Tech industry security

# 4. Threat Intelligence & Research Organizations

## Citizen Lab
- University of Toronto research group
- Surveillance research
- Government spyware investigations

## Intel 471
- Cybercrime intelligence company
- Criminal underground monitoring

## The Record (Recorded Future)
- Cybersecurity news arm of Recorded Future
- Threat intelligence reporting

## Securelist (Kaspersky)
- Research blog by Kaspersky
- APT reports
- Malware analysis

## CISA
- U.S. Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency
- Publishes advisories
- CVE alerts
- Incident response guidance

## CERN (Computer Security)
- Protects global scientific infrastructure
- Advanced network defense

## KBCERT
- National CERT of South Korea
- Incident response and vulnerability disclosure

# 8. APT & Threat Reports

APT Reports typically include:
- Attribution analysis
- TTP mapping (MITRE ATT&CK)
- Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)
- Malware reverse engineering
- Infrastructure tracking

Organizations publishing APT reports:
- Kaspersky (Securelist)
- Insikt Group
- Citizen Lab
- Flashpoint
- DarkOwl
- Intel 471
