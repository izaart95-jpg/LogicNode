📡 Understanding CSI Motion Detection

What is Movement Score?

The Movement Score represents the magnitude of change detected in the Wi-Fi Channel State Information (CSI). When someone moves in the monitored area, they disturb the Wi-Fi signal patterns, causing fluctuations in the CSI data. The system analyzes these changes using the MVS (Mean Variance Shift) detector algorithm.

How the Threshold Works

The system uses an automatic threshold of 3.13 (P95×1.4). When the Movement Score exceeds this threshold, motion is detected. This threshold is calculated based on the 95th percentile of baseline readings multiplied by 1.4 to reduce false positives.

Reading the Dashboard

  • Movement Score: Real-time detection value. Higher = more motion detected
  • State: IDLE (no motion) or MOTION (movement detected above threshold)
  • Packet Rate: Wi-Fi packets analyzed per second (~100 pps typical)
  • RSSI: Received Signal Strength Indicator. -30 to -60 dBm is excellent, -60 to -70 is good
  • Percentage: Movement score as percentage of threshold (100% = at threshold)

CSI Technology Explained

Channel State Information (CSI) captures how Wi-Fi signals propagate through space. Unlike simple RSSI-based detection, CSI analyzes multiple subcarriers (channels 11-15, 46-52) providing fine-grained information about signal phase and amplitude changes. This makes it highly sensitive to human movement while being relatively immune to environmental changes.

Optimal Setup Tips

  • Place the ESP32 device in a central location with clear line-of-sight to the monitored area
  • Avoid placing near large metal objects or behind walls
  • Maintain good Wi-Fi signal strength (RSSI > -70 dBm) for best accuracy
  • The system adapts its baseline automatically - allow 1-2 minutes for calibration
  • Typical detection range: 5-10 meters depending on environment
